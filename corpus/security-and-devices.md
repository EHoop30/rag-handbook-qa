# Security and Devices

## Passwords and Multi-Factor Authentication

All accounts must use a password of at least 14 characters. Passwords must be
unique to Meridian systems and stored in the company password manager.
Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required on every system that supports it,
including email, the VPN, and cloud consoles. Hardware security keys are
provided on request and are required for administrator accounts.

## Device Security

Every laptop must have full-disk encryption enabled and the company endpoint
agent installed. Devices must lock automatically after 10 minutes of
inactivity. Lost or stolen devices must be reported to the IT team immediately
so the device can be remotely wiped.

## Handling Company and Customer Data

Customer data may only be stored in approved company systems. It must never be
copied to personal devices, personal cloud storage, or personal email.
Production credentials and API keys must be stored in the company secrets
manager, never in code, configuration files, or chat messages.

## Reporting a Security Incident

Suspected security incidents, including phishing, malware, and lost devices,
must be reported to security@meridian.example within 1 hour of discovery.
Reporting a mistake in good faith is always encouraged and never penalized;
early reporting is what limits the damage of an incident.

## Access and Least Privilege

Access to systems is granted on a least-privilege basis. Employees receive the
minimum access needed for their role, and access is reviewed quarterly. Access
is removed the same day an employee changes roles or leaves the company.
